Percentages

We use percentages to measure part of a total amount using a fraction or decimal.

If we know the part and the total amount, we divide to find the percent.

  • What percent of 200 is 160? $$ 160 / 200 = 0.8 = 80\% = \frac{80}{100}$$
  • What percent of 136 is 8? $$ \frac{8}{136} = 0.0588 = 5.88\% = \frac{5.88}{100} $$

To write a percent with the % sign, we move the decimal two places.

$$ 0.07 = 7\% \qquad 0.52 = 52\% \qquad 1.25 = 125\% $$

Percentages of an amount

To find a given percentage of an amount, we convert the percent to a decimal and them multiply.

  • What is 25% of 170? $$ 0.25 * 170 = 42.5 $$
  • What is 140% of 16? $$ 1.40 * 16 - 22.4 $$

Percent change

Percent change is a way to express how much something has changed, as a percentage of the original amount. To compute percent change, we compute

$$ \text{Percent change} = \frac{\text{Amount of change}}{\text{Original amount}}$$
  • If the value of my house goes from 120,000 to 130,000, what is the percent change?
    • The amount of change is 10,000
    • The percent change is $$ \frac{10000}{120000} = 0.083 = 8.3\%$$
  • If the cost of an item decreases from 5.00 to 4.25, what is the percent change?
    • The amount of change is -0.75. The amount is negative because the cost went down.
    • The percent change is $$ \frac{-0.75}{5.00} = -0.15 = -15\%$$
