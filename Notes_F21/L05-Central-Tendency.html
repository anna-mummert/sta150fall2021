Central Tendency

We use the term central tendency to describe the "average" or "typical" value of a data set.

There are several ways to talk about the average, which apply in different situations.

Mode

For categorical data, there are not may ways to measure the “center”.

The most common data value is called the mode. In some cases, this is an informative measure.

  • The most common college for STA 150 students is the College of Business.
  • The most common family name in South Korea is Kim, with about 21.5 percent of their population. The next most common, Lee, isused by about 14.7 percent of the population.

For scale data, we don't normally talk about the mode. We use other measures.

Median

The median represents the middle of the data when it is sorted in order.

When there are an odd number of data points, the median is the middle value.

  • The median of 12, 14, 17, 19, 21 is 17
  • The median of 9, 9, 10, 12, 12, 13, 15, 17, 19 is 12

When there are an even number of data points, the median is halfway between the middle two points.

  • The median of 20, 30, 40, 50 is 35, halfway between 30 and 40.
  • The median of 3, 4, 4, 6, 8, 10 is 5, halfway between 4 and 6.

Mean

The mean of a data set is made by adding the values and dividing by the number of values.

  • For the data set 4, 8, 10, 14, the total is 36.
    The mean is 36 / 4 = 9.
  • For the data set 20, 25, 30, 45, 50, the total is 170, and the mean is 170 / 5 = 34.

Notation for the mean

In statistics, we use special notation to describe some of our computations.

We let $N$ stand for the size of a sample or data set.

We use variables like $x$ and $y$ to stand for the data. These are sometimes called random variables.

In algebra, a variable stands for a single number. In statistics, a variable stands for the entire set of data.

Notation for the mean

We use the $\sum$ symbol to mean “Add these values”.

So, if $x$ stands for a data set, the notation \[ \sum x \] means to add the values of the data set.

In this notation, the mean value of a data set is \[ \frac{\sum x}{N} \]

Notation for the mean

Because the mean is used so often, it has special notation.

If $x$ stands for a data set, we let $\bar x$ represent the mean of the data set.

So we have the formula \[ \bar x = \frac{\sum x}{N} \]

Sensitivity to outliers

Outliers are data points that are very different from the others – much larger or much smaller.

For example, consider a group of people whose heights, in inches, are 62, 62, 64, 66, and 78.

The person who is 78 inches tall is an outlier in this group.

The median height is 64. Half the group is shorter than the median, and half is taller than the median.

The mean height is 68. All but one person in the group is shorter than the mean height.

Outliers example

To show the effect of outliers, we will look at data from the Congressional Budget Office ( The Distribution of Household Income, 2016 released in July 2019).

The CBO divided the population into 5 groups, each of which have the same number of households. The population was divided by household income after taxes and transfers were taken into account.

So these numbers represent income after taxes, but including benefits such as SNAP, Medicaid, etc.

2016 U.S. Household Income Data

Each group includes the same number of households.

Average income of each group of households:

  • First 20%: $35,000
  • Second 20%: $48,000
  • Third 20%: $66,000
  • Fourth 20%: $92,000
  • Fifth 20%: $215,000

Comparing the median and mean

The median U.S. household income in 2016 from this data was $66,000.

The mean U.S. household income in 2016 from this data was $91,200.

The median represents a line halfway from the least household income to the highest.

The mean is the number that you would multiply by the number of households to find the total household income in the U.S.

Summary

Overall, the median is not very sensitive to outliers.

For example, if we throw out the same number of data points at the top and bottom of the data, the median is unchanged.

The mean is relatively sensitive to outliers. A large change in just a few data points can cause notable changes in the mean.

Normally distributed data

Later in the course, we will see that many data sets have what is called a normal distribution:

  • Heights of people in a large population
  • Weights of birds in a large flock of birds
  • Scores of a large population on a standardized exam

For normally distributed data, the median and the mean will be the same, or almost the same.
