Estimating the Mean

We want to use the sample data to estimate the population mean.


The sample mean is a point estimate for the population mean.


We'll extend our point estimate to an interval of values called a confidence interval.

  • The confidence interval will be centered at the mean.
  • The confidence interval may or may not actually contain the population mean.
  • If we could repeatedly take samples, compute the confidence intervals, then most of the confidence intervals should contain the population mean.

Central Limit Theorem

From a population, take a sample of size $n$. Compute the mean.

  • The mean of the sample is a new "individual" in a new population

Repeatedly take samples of size $n$; compute the means.

The set of all the sample means (all the individuals in the population) forms the sampling distribution.

The mean and standard deviation of the original population and the sampling distribution are related

  • Population mean $\mu$, standard deviation $\sigma$
  • Sampling distribution mean $\mu$, standard deviation $\dfrac{\sigma}{\sqrt{n}}$

If the original population is normally distributed or the sample size $n \geq 30$

  • The sampling distribution will be normally distributed

Centered Regions

The confidence interval will be centered at the mean.

We're looking for centered regions on a normal distribution (sampling distribution is normal).

We'll find the critical value $c$, and the interval will be of the form \[P(-c < z < c) \]

Usually we pick $c$ so that $90\%$ or $95\%$ of the data is in the region. \[P(-c < z < c) = 0.9 \quad \text{or}\quad P(-c < z < c) = 0.95 \]

Notation: $c$ is a $z$ value; it can be written as $z_c$


Check your understanding

Find the exact areas (probabilities) of these centered regions. Use a $Z$ table.

  • The boundaries of a centered region are one standard deviation from mean.
    • $z = -1$ has $P(z < -1) = 0.1587$
    • area of both tails is $2 * 0.1587 = 0.3174$
    • inside centered region (area not in tails) $A = 1- 0.3174 = 0.6826$
  • The boundaries of a centered region are two standard deviation from mean.
    • $z = -2$ has $P(z < -2) = 0.0228$
    • area of both tails is $2 * 0.0228 = 0.0456$
    • inside centered region (area not in tails) $A = 1- 0.0456 = 0.9544$
  • The boundaries of a centered region are three standard deviation from mean.
    • $z = -3$ has $P(z < -3) = 0.0013$
    • area of both tails is $2 * 0.0013 = 0.0026$
    • inside centered region (area not in tails) $A = 1- 0.0026 = 0.9974$

Critical Value

Calculating $c$ so region has area $A$

  • Compute \[ \frac{1-A}{2}\]
  • Find the $z$ value that corresponds to this number; this is $-c$

Example: Find the boundaries of a centered region with area $A = 85\%$.

Compute \[ \frac{1-0.85}{2} = 0.075\]

Look up $z$ value: $z = -1.44$

Thus, $c = 1.44$ and the boundaries are $\pm 1.44$

Approximate Critical Values

Find the boundaries of a centered region with area $A = 68\%$

  • $c = 1$; boundaries $\pm 1$ -- one standard deviation

Find the boundaries of a centered region with area $A = 95\%$

  • $c = 2$; boundaries $\pm 2$ -- two standard deviation

Find the boundaries of a centered region with area $A = 99.7\%$

  • $c = 3$; boundaries $\pm 3$ -- three standard deviation
