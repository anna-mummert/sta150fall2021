Confidence Intervals for the Mean

Estimating the Mean

We want to use the sample data to estimate the population mean.


The sample mean is a point estimate for the population mean.


We'll extend our point estimate to an interval of values called a confidence interval.

  • The confidence interval will be centered at the mean.
  • The confidence interval may or may not actually contain the population mean.
  • If we could repeatedly take samples, compute the confidence intervals, then most of the confidence intervals should contain the population mean.

Assumptions

Sample size $n$ and population standard deviation is $\sigma$

Assume either (1) the population is normally distributed or (2) $n \geq 30$

The sampling distribution has mean $\mu$ and standard deviation $\dfrac{\sigma}{\sqrt{n}}$

Centered Regions

The confidence interval will be centered at the sample mean $\overline{x}$.

We're looking for centered regions on a normal distribution (sampling distribution is normal).

We'll find the critical value $c$ (or $z_c$), and the interval will be of the form \[P(-c < z < c) \]

Convert the interval of $z$ values to an interval of $x$ values

The margin of error is \[E = z_c \left(\frac{\sigma}{\sqrt{n}} \right) \]

The confidence interval is $(\overline{x} - E, \overline{x}+E)$

Example

We previously found that the critical value for $85\%$ confidence level is $z_c = 1.44$.

Suppose we know a distribution is normal and has a standard deviation of 5.

We take a sample of size 49, and our sample average is 37.

Here's what we can say

  • The point estimate for the population mean is 37.
  • The margin of error for $85\%$ confidence interval is \[E = 1.44 \left(\frac{5}{\sqrt{49}}\right) = 1.02 \]
  • The $85\%$ confidence interval is $(37 - 1.02, 37 + 1.02)$, that is \[(35.98, 38.02) \]

Confidence Interval Meaning

If we could repeatedly take samples, compute the confidence intervals, then most of the confidence intervals should contain the population mean.

Every confidence interval will have the same width (because the sample size is the same $n$)

For our $85\%$ confidence interval, we say

  • We are $85\%$ confident that the unknown population mean is between 35.98 and 38.02.
  • $85\%$ of confidence intervals will contain the true population mean.
  • $1 - 85\% = 15\%$ of confidence intervals will not contain the true population mean.

Check your understanding

  • A data set has sample mean $\overline{x} = 15$ and $95\%$ margin of error $E = 3.2$. Find the $95\%$ confidence interval.
    • $(15 - 3.2 , 15 + 3.2) = (11.8, 18.2)$
  • True or false: With all other things equal, if your confidence level is larger, then the margin of error will be larger, too.
    • True
  • True or false: With all other things equal, if your standard deviation is larger, then the margin of error will be larger, too.
    • True
  • True or false: With all other things equal, if your sample size is larger, then the margin of error will be larger, too.
    • False
