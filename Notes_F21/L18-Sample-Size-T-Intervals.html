Sample Size

Find the sample size needed to achieve a certain confidence interval

The general formal for the sample size is \[n = \left(\frac{z_c \sigma}{E}\right)^2 \]

Round up to the next whole number

Example

A population has standard deviation 5.

How large a sample size do we need to find a margin of error $E$ of 0.75?

Use a $85\%$ confidence interval.


Find the critical value $z_c$: $z_c = 1.44$

Use the formula \[n = \left(\frac{1.44 * 5}{0.75}\right)^2 = 92.16\]

We need to have a sample of size $n = 93$

Check your understanding

  • True or false: With all other things equal, if your confidence level is larger, then the sample size will be larger, too.
    • True
  • True or false: With all other things equal, if your standard deviation is larger, then the sample size will be larger, too.
    • True
  • True or false: With all other things equal, if your margin of error is larger, then the sample size will be larger, too.
    • False

unknown Standard Deviation or small sample sizes

Using the normal distribution works well for (1) known population standard deviation or (2) sample size $n \geq 30$

These conditions are violated frequently in real world applications

To compute confidence intervals in this case (unknown standard deviation or small sample size)

  • Use the sample standard deviation $s$ as a point estimate for the population standard deviation
  • Use the Student's t-distribution, instead of the normal distribution

Student's t-distribution

The distribution shape depends on the sample size $n$.

  • The degrees of freedom is $df = n-1$

All Student's t-distributions are unimodal (mound shaped) and symmetric; they have fatter tails than a normal distribution

The critical values $t_c$ are found from a T table

  • Find the row for your degrees of freedom $df$
  • Find the column for your confidence level $c$

Confidence intervals

Compute the sample mean $\overline{x}$ and sample standard deviation $s$

Compute the degrees of freedom $df = n-1$

Find the critical value $t_c$ for your confidence level $c$

Compute the margin of error \[ E = t_c \left(\frac{s}{\sqrt{n}} \right)\]

The confidence interval is \[(\overline{x} - E, \overline{x} + E) \]

Example

You collect a sample of size 12 with a sample standard deviation of 55 and a sample average of 489.

Find the $85\%$ confidence interval for the mean


The degrees of freedom is $df = 11$

The critical value is $t_c = 1.548$

The margin of error is \[ E = 1.548 \left(\frac{55}{\sqrt{12}} \right) = 24.58\]

The $85\%$ confidence interval is $(489 - 24.58, 489 + 24.58)$, that is \[(464.42, 513.58) \]

Check your understanding

  • True or false: The shape of a normal distribution depends on the sample size.
    • False
  • True or false: The degrees of freedom depend on the sample size.
    • True. $df = n-1$
  • True or false: The critical value $t_c$ increases as the degrees of freedom increase.
    • False
  • True or false: The critical value $t_c$ increases as the level of confidence increases.
    • True
