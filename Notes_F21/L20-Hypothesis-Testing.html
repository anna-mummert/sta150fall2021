Hypothesis Testing

General process

  • Identify the hypothesis you want to test. Null hypothesis the two values are equal. Alternative hypothesis the values are different $>$, $<$, or $\neq$.
  • Identify the level of significance $\alpha$ you want to use.
  • Convert the sample statistic into a $z$-value
  • Compute the probability $p$-value of getting a sample as extreme or more extreme than our sample.
  • If the probability is lower than the level of significance we say reject the null hypothesis and accept the alternative hypothesis ($p < \alpha$). Otherwise, we say the evidence is not strong enough to reject the null hypothesis ($p > \alpha$).

One-sided and Two-sided Tests

One-sided test -- the alternative hypothesis is "less than" or "greater than".

  • For "less than", read the $p$-value directly from the $Z$ table
  • For "greater than", find the probability and subtract : $1 - p$.

Two-sided test -- the alternative hypothesis is "not equal to"

  • If $z < 0$, find the probability and multiply by 2 : $2p$.
  • If $z > 0$, find the probability, subtract, and multiply by 2 : $2(1-p)$.

Example

The mean work week for engineers in a company is believed to be about 60 hours. A newly hired engineer hopes that it's shorter. The work week of 10 engineers from the company are \[45 \quad 50 \quad 55 \quad 55 \quad 55 \quad 55 \quad 60 \quad 60 \quad 65\quad 70 \] Based on the data should the new engineer count on the mean work week to be shorter than 60 hours?

The length of engineer work weeks at this company is known to be normally distributed with a standard deviation of 6 hours.

Use a level of significance $\alpha = 0.1$

Example

Null hypothesis: $H_0: \mu = 60$ hours

Alternative hypothesis: $H_1: \mu < 60$ hours

Compute the $z$-value: $z = -1.58$

Compute the $p$-value: $P(z < -1.58) = 0.0571$

Compare $p$ and $\alpha$: $0.0571 < 0.1$

Because the $p$ value is less than the significance level $\alpha$, we reject the null hypothesis and accept the alternative hypothesis.

The data supports the claim that the average work week at this company is less than 60 hours.

Example

It is believed that a stock price for a particular company will grow at a rate of $\$5$ per week with a standard deviation of $\$2$. An investor does not believe this claim.

The changes in stock price is recorded for ten weeks and are as follows \[2 \quad 3 \quad 3 \quad 4 \quad 5 \quad 5 \quad 6 \quad 7\quad 8 \quad 10 \]

It is believed that the growth rate of the stock is normally distributed.

Perform a hypothesis test using a $5\%$ level of significance.

Example

Null hypothesis: $H_0: \mu = 5$ dollars

Alternative hypothesis: $H_1: \mu \neq 5$ dollars

Compute the sample mean $\overline{x} = 5.3$

Compute the $z$-value \[ z = (5.3 - 5)/ \left(\frac{2}{\sqrt{10}}\right) = 0.47\]

From the $Z$ table, $P(z < 0.47) = 0.6808$

Example

From the $Z$ table, $P(z < 0.47) = 0.6808$

Subtract from $1$ to find the probability of another sample having a mean larger than our sample mean $5.3$. \[1 - 0.6808 = 0.3152\]

Multiply by two to find the $p$-value: $p = 0.6304$

Compare the $p$-value with the significance level $\alpha$ \[0.6304 > 0.05 \]

Do not reject the null hypothesis. The evidence supports the claim that the weekly growth rate of the stock is $\$5$.

Check your understanding

Make a conclusion for each of the hypothesis tests at the $5\%$ level of significance.

  • We want to test whether the mean height of eighth graders is 66 inches.
    The null and alternative hypothese are
    $H_0: \mu = 66$ inches and $H_1: \mu \neq 66$ inches.
    You compute the $p$-value and find $p = 0.46$.
    • Do not reject the null hypothesis. The data supports the claim that the mean height of eighth graders is 66 inches.
  • We want to test if college students take less than five years to graduate from college, on the average.
    The null and alternative hypotheses are
    $H_0: \mu = 5$ years and $H_1: \mu < 5$ years.
    You compute the $p$-value and find $p = 0.02$.
    • Reject the null hypothesis. The data supports the claim that, on average, college students take less than five years to graduate from college.
