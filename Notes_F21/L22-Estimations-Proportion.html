Estimating Proportions

We want to use the sample data to estimate the population proportion.


The sample proportion is a point estimate for the population proportion. $\hat p = \dfrac{r}{n} $


We'll extend our point estimate to an interval of values called a confidence interval.

  • The confidence interval will be centered at the sample proportion.
  • The confidence interval may or may not actually contain the population proportion.
  • If we could repeatedly take samples, compute the confidence intervals, then most of the confidence intervals should contain the population proportion.

Proportions follows a Normal Distribution

When $n$ is large enough and $p$ is not close to $0$ or $1$, the population proportion is normally distributed

  • mean $p$
  • standard deviation $\frac{\sqrt{p(1-p)}}{\sqrt{n}}$

Confidence Intervals

Because the standard deviation is known we can use normal distributions to find the confidence intervals.

Use $Z$ tables to find the critical values, $z_c$

The margin of error is \[E = z_c \left(\frac{\sqrt{p(1-p)}}{\sqrt{n}} \right)\]

The confidence interval is $(\hat p - E, \hat p +E)$

Example

A market research firm randomly selected 500 residents in a city to determine whether they have cell phones.

Of the 500 people surveyed, 421 responded yes they own a cell phone.

Using an $85\%$ confidence level, compute a confidence interval estimate for the true proportion of residents in the city who have a cell phone.

Example

Of the 500 people surveyed, 421 responded yes they own a cell phone.

The point estimate for the proportion is $\hat{p} = \dfrac{421}{500} = 0.842$

The critical value is $z_c = 1.44$ \[\frac{1 - 0.85}{2} = 0.075, \quad \text{then look up } z \text{ value} \]

The margin of error is \[ E = 1.44 \left(\frac{\sqrt{0.842(1-0.842)}}{\sqrt{500}} \right) = 0.0236\]

Example

The $85\%$ confidence interval is $(0.842 - 0.0236, 0.842 + 0.0236)$, that is \[ (0.8184, 0.8656) \]

With $85\%$ confidence, between $81.84\%$ and $86.56\%$ of residents in the city own a cell phone.

$85\%$ of all confidence intervals constructed in this way would contain the true population proportion.

$1 - 85\% = 15\%$ of confidence intervals will not contain the true population proportion.

Check your understanding

Suppose 250 randomly selected people are surveyed to determine if they own a tablet. Of the 250 surveyed, 98 reported owning a tablet. Using a 95% confidence level, compute a confidence interval estimate for the true proportion of peole who own tablets.

  • Compute the sample proportion of those who own tablets.
    • $\hat{p} = 98/250 = 0.392 = 39.2\%$
  • Find the critical value $z_c$ for the $95\%$ confidence interval.
    • $z_c = 1.96$
  • What is the margin of error $E$ for our $95\%$ confidence interval?
    • \[ E = 1.96 \left(\frac{\sqrt{0.392(1-0.392)}}{\sqrt{250}} \right) = 0.0605\]
  • What is our $95\%$ confidence interval for the population proprotion who own tablets?
    • $(0.392 - 0.0605, 0.392 + 0.0605) = (0.3315, 0.4525)$
