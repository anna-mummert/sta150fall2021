Hypothesis Testing

General process -- same as for mean with known standard deviation ($z$-test)

  • Identify the hypothesis you want to test. Null hypothesis the two values are equal. Alternative hypothesis the values are different $>$, $<$, or $\neq$.
  • Identify the level of significance $\alpha$ you want to use.
  • Convert the sample statistic into a $z$-value: \[\displaystyle{z = (\hat{p} - p) / \left( \frac{\sqrt{p(1-p)}}{\sqrt{n}}\right)}\]
  • Compute the probability $p$-value of getting a sample as extreme or more extreme than our sample.
  • If the probability is lower than the level of significance we say reject the null hypothesis and accept the alternative hypothesis ($p < \alpha$). Otherwise, we say the evidence is not strong enough to reject the null hypothesis ($p > \alpha$).

Example

A market research firm believes that more than $88\%$ of residents in a city own a cell phone.

The firm randomly selected 500 residents in a city to determine whether they have cell phones.

Of the 500 people surveyed, 421 responded yes they own a cell phone.

Test the claim using a hypothesis test. Use a $10\%$ level of significance .

Example

Null hypothesis: $H_0: p = 88\%$

Alternative hypothesis: $H_1: p > 88\%$

Compute the sample proportion: $\hat{p} = 421 / 500 = 0.842$

Compute the $z$-value: $z = -2.61$ \[\displaystyle{z = (0.842 - 0.88) / \left( \frac{\sqrt{0.88(1-0.88)}}{\sqrt{500}}\right) = -2.61}\]

Example

Compute the $p$-value: $P(z > -2.61) = 1 - 0.0045 = 0.9955$

Compare $p$ and $\alpha$: $0.9955 > 0.1$

Because the $p$ value is greater than the significance level $\alpha$, we do reject the null hypothesis.

The data does not support the claim that more than $88\%$ of residents own a cell phone.

Check your understanding

You suspect that more than $33\%$ of people own a tablet. Of 250 randomly selected people surveyed, 98 reported owning a tablet. Use a $5\%$ level of significance and perform a hypothesis test on this claim

  • State null and alternative hypotheses.
    • $H_0: p = 0.33$ and $H_1: p > 0.33$
  • Find the $z$ value.
    • \[\displaystyle{z = (0.392 - 0.33) / \left( \frac{\sqrt{0.33(1-0.33)}}{\sqrt{250}}\right)}=2.08\]
  • Compute the $p$-value.
    • $P(z > 2.08) = 1 - 0.9812 = 0.0188$
  • What is your conclusion?
    • Reject the null hypothesis. The data supports the claim that more than $33\%$ of people own a tablet.
